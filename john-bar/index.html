Euro 2020 Review

As a diehard soccer fan, I can’t help but notice the lack of scoring in close games at this year's European Championship. Each major tournament is a chance for at least one forward, goalie, midfielder, and increasingly often outside backs, to make a big money move after impressing on the international stage. I wondered with my friends who it’d be this year, my hope was that Alex Isak the lengthy young forward from Sweden would set the group stages ablaze and rally that into a big time transfer.


Those of you who know me, know I’m a huge Liverpool fan. Watching this year's European Championship was like watching Liverpool’s 2020/2021 campaign all over again. Missing clear cut chances, converting penalties, own goals and clutch goals. The brand was never consistent and the poor string of results showed.


Something has happened over the past few years, Messi and Ronaldo scored non-stop, Lewandoski comes close, but the age of elite goal scorers seems to have passed us by. Mbappe is the aire apparent but Haaland scores more, and neither seem as invincible as Ronaldo and Messi on their ascents to superstardom. Before Ronaldo and Messi, we had elite strikers all over Europe vying for that top spot.

Didier Drogba’s 2012 Champions League win was the last time I can remember a top forward winning the title. Drogba was the last of a dying breed of poachers and it shows with the missed chances we see in the game today.
